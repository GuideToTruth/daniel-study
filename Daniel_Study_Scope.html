Purpose of This Study

We live in a world asking urgent questions. Wars and rumours of war. Conflict in the Middle East. The war with Iran. Economic pressure. Nations in turmoil. These are not random events — they are signs. And the Book of Daniel was written precisely for people living in times like these.

Our goal is simple: let the Bible interpret itself. No outside theories, no speculation — just Scripture explaining Scripture, with clear connections to prophecy, history, and the world we live in today. We want every person in this study — whether brand new to the Bible or a lifelong student — to leave with a clearer understanding of where history is heading and the confidence that God is still on the throne.

Chapter-by-Chapter Scope

Complete Book of Daniel · 12 Chapters · 10 Sessions
Part 1 — The Historical Chapters · Daniel 1–6

These chapters tell the story of Daniel and his friends living faithfully in a foreign empire. Each story is also a prophetic type — a picture of God's people in the last days.

1

Daniel 1

Faith Under Fire in Babylon
Key verse: Daniel 1:8 — "But Daniel purposed in his heart…"
The Babylonian captivity. Nebuchadnezzar's identity programme — changing names, diet, and education. Daniel's decision to remain faithful. The 10-day test. God honours those who honour Him. God gives Daniel understanding in visions and dreams — the gift that carries the whole book.
Connects to: Lev. 11 · Jer. 25:11 · Rev. 13–14 · Matt. 4:4
▶ Session 1
2

Daniel 2

The Dream That Mapped World History
Key verse: Daniel 2:44 — "And in the days of these kings shall the God of heaven set up a kingdom…"
Nebuchadnezzar's great image. Gold (Babylon) · Silver (Persia) · Bronze (Greece) · Iron (Rome) · Iron and clay (divided Europe). The stone not cut by human hands — God's eternal Kingdom. The most accessible prophetic overview in Scripture. This chapter is the master key to all prophetic chapters that follow.
Connects to: Dan. 7 · Dan. 8 · Rev. 17 · Matt. 21:44
Session 2
3

Daniel 3

The Fiery Furnace
Key verse: Daniel 3:17–18 — "Our God whom we serve is able to deliver us… but if not…"
Nebuchadnezzar builds a golden image — the same dimensions as the image of Dan. 2, but all gold. The empire-wide worship decree. Three men who refuse to bow. The fourth man in the fire. Faith that does not negotiate even under a death sentence. Type of the final Sunday law and end-time worship decree.
Connects to: Rev. 13:15 · Rev. 14:9–11 · Ex. 20:3–5
Session 3
4

Daniel 4

Nebuchadnezzar's Humbling
Key verse: Daniel 4:37 — "Those that walk in pride He is able to abase."
The great tree dream. Pride and its consequences. Seven times of madness. The most powerful man on earth brought to his knees. Nebuchadnezzar's eventual acknowledgment of God. The lesson that God rules in the kingdom of men. A warning to all earthly powers — relevant to the pride of modern nations.
Connects to: Prov. 16:18 · Isa. 14:12–15 · Rev. 18:7
Session 4
5

Daniel 5

The Handwriting on the Wall
Key verse: Daniel 5:27 — "Tekel; Thou art weighed in the balances, and art found wanting."
Belshazzar's feast. The desecration of the temple vessels. The mysterious hand writing: MENE MENE TEKEL UPHARSIN. Daniel's courage to speak truth to power. Babylon falls that very night to the Medes and Persians. The transition from Babylon to Persia — exactly as Daniel 2 predicted.
Connects to: Isa. 21:9 · Dan. 2:39 · Rev. 18:2
Session 4
6

Daniel 6

The Lions' Den
Key verse: Daniel 6:10 — "…he kneeled upon his knees three times a day, and prayed…"
Daniel's prayer life — the foundation of his faithfulness. The empire-wide prayer ban. The trap set by his enemies. Daniel's habit of prayer does not change when it becomes illegal. The lions' mouths shut. Darius's decree. Type of the end-time Sunday law and the remnant who keep the Sabbath.
Connects to: Rev. 13:16–17 · Acts 5:29 · Ps. 55:17
Session 5
Part 2 — The Prophetic Chapters · Daniel 7–12

These chapters contain the major prophetic visions of Daniel. They cover the same ground as chapters 1–6 but go deeper — revealing the judgment, the sanctuary, the Messiah, the great controversy, and the time of the end.

7

Daniel 7

The Judgment Scene in Heaven
Key verse: Daniel 7:9–10 — "I beheld till the thrones were cast down, and the Ancient of Days did sit…"
Four beasts = same four kingdoms as Daniel 2 but with more detail. The little horn power — its identity and timeline. The pre-Advent judgment (investigative judgment). The Ancient of Days takes His seat. The Son of Man brought before Him. The saints receive the Kingdom. The 1844 connection begins here.
Connects to: Dan. 2 · Dan. 8 · Rev. 13 · Heb. 8:1–2 · Rev. 14:6–7
Session 6
8

Daniel 8

The 2,300-Day Prophecy
Key verse: Daniel 8:14 — "Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed."
Ram (Persia) · Goat (Greece) · Little horn (Rome, both pagan and papal). The casting down of the sanctuary. The longest time prophecy in all of Scripture — 2,300 prophetic days. Daniel is overwhelmed and the vision is sealed. The answer to "when?" is given in Daniel 9.
Connects to: Dan. 9 · Num. 14:34 · Ezek. 4:6 · Heb. 9:23
Session 7
9

Daniel 9

The 70 Weeks and the Messiah
Key verse: Daniel 9:25 — "Know therefore and understand, that from the going forth of the commandment to restore… unto the Messiah the Prince shall be seven weeks…"
Daniel's prayer of repentance. Gabriel's answer: 70 weeks (490 years) are cut off from the 2,300 days. Starting point: 457 BC decree of Artaxerxes. Pinpoints Christ's baptism (27 AD) and crucifixion (31 AD) with stunning precision. The confirming of the covenant. The end of the 490 years (34 AD). The key that unlocks Daniel 8.
Connects to: Dan. 8:14 · Ezra 7 · Matt. 3:13–17 · Luke 3:1 · Gal. 3:16–17
Session 8
10

Daniel 10

The Great Controversy Backdrop
Key verse: Daniel 10:13 — "But the prince of the kingdom of Persia withstood me one and twenty days… but, lo, Michael, one of the chief princes, came to help me."
Daniel's 21 days of fasting and prayer. The heavenly messenger delayed by spiritual conflict. Michael (Christ) comes to help. A glimpse behind the curtain of history — the real battle is spiritual. Sets the context for the great controversy between Christ and Satan across chapters 11–12.
Connects to: Eph. 6:12 · Rev. 12:7 · Jude 9 · 1 Thess. 4:16
Session 9
11

Daniel 11

Kings of the North and South
Key verse: Daniel 11:45 — "And he shall plant the tabernacles of his palace… yet he shall come to his end, and none shall help him."
The most detailed prophetic chapter in the Bible. Persia, Greece, Rome, and the divided powers. The king of the North and the king of the South — a centuries-long conflict that reaches into our time. Papal Rome, the time of the end, and the final movements. Connects directly to current geopolitical events.
Connects to: Dan. 7 · Dan. 8 · Rev. 16 · 2 Thess. 2:3–4
Session 9
12

Daniel 12

The Time of the End
Key verse: Daniel 12:1 — "And at that time shall Michael stand up… and there shall be a time of trouble, such as never was…"
Michael stands up. The great time of trouble. Every one whose name is written in the book shall be delivered. The resurrection promise. Two more time prophecies: 1,260 · 1,290 · 1,335 days. The book sealed until the time of the end. "Blessed is he that waiteth" — the final note of hope.
Connects to: Rev. 3:5 · 1 Thess. 4:16–17 · Matt. 24:21 · Rev. 22:11–12
Session 10

The Prophetic Chain — How the Chapters Connect

Daniel's prophetic chapters are not isolated visions. They build on each other like a chain, each one adding detail to the one before.

Dan. 2The overview — 4 kingdoms + God's Kingdom
→
Dan. 7Same kingdoms + adds the little horn + the judgment
→
Dan. 8Focuses on the little horn + 2,300 days
→
Dan. 9Unlocks 2,300 days with 70-week key + Messiah's timeline
→
Dan. 10–12The great controversy + detailed end-time events + resurrection
